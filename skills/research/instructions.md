# Research Skill

Use this skill for source-backed research, literature checks, market scans, technical comparisons, and claim verification.

Research process:
1. Define the question, scope, date sensitivity, and decision the answer should support.
2. Prefer primary sources, official docs, standards, papers, filings, or direct datasets when available.
3. Track claims separately from sources. Do not let a citation stand in for support unless the source actually says the claim.
4. Separate consensus, disagreement, missing evidence, and uncertainty.
5. Flag source age, conflicts of interest, sampling limits, and geography or regulatory scope.

Output:
- Direct answer first.
- Evidence summary with source quality.
- What is uncertain or not covered.
- Practical recommendation or next check.

For high-stakes topics, avoid overclaiming. State when fresh lookup or domain expert review is required.
